Working remotely

Time saving vs coordinated rest Remote work allows you to save up to 2 hours on the way to the office and back. Saving time is a plus. However, for some people, a trip to the office is the very minutes or even hours in which you can escape from everyday and work problems. This forced "disconnection" from worries makes it possible to be alone and reboot the brain. On the other hand, this time could be allocated and consciously, when and how you need it. But psychologically it's a little more complicated. Individual environment vs ready-made resources. Another point of remote work, which was initially attributed to the advantages, is the opportunity to work in a familiar environment, on convenient equipment, as well as eat homemade food. But someone will immediately say that the home environment may not always be conducive to work, and homemade food still needs to be cooked, and working equipment needs to be taken from somewhere. And as a result, some are happy with the freedom of choice and begin to equip the place as they are comfortable and their productivity will melt. Others do not want to equip anything and just accumulate discontent.

Office work

Advantages of office work: Excellent self-organization. Unlike homework, you have everything scheduled by the minute. There is a certain time when you have to start your duties, time has been allocated for a lunch break and it is known at what time it is possible to go home.
